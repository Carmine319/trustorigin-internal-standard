# Continuity Risk

## Definition

Continuity Risk is the structural failure mode whereby governance-critical artefacts lose traceability, lineage coherence, export survivability or dependency clarity over time.

It is not misconduct.
It is not regulatory breach.
It is structural erosion.

Continuity Risk frequently precedes:

- Litigation escalation
- Regulatory exposure
- Insurance dispute amplification
- Institutional instability

---

## Structural Characteristics

Continuity Risk emerges when:

- Revision histories cannot be deterministically reconstructed
- Export survivability is not guaranteed
- Vendor dependencies are undocumented
- Freeze states cannot be activated during dispute
- Amendment lineage lacks structural enforcement

---

## Infrastructure Position

TrustOrigin mitigates Continuity Risk through deterministic serialisation, lineage enforcement and export survivability frameworks.

The platform does not adjudicate disputes.
It preserves structural continuity posture.
