# TrustOrigin Internal Standard

This repository publishes the **TrustOrigin Internal Standard** for a neutral,
non-adjudicative registry infrastructure.

The standard defines mandatory governance, operational boundaries, and
non-claims principles governing the TrustOrigin registry.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this repository is to make the TrustOrigin Internal Standard
publicly accessible, citable, and reviewable.

This repository does **not** provide software, validation tools, or enforcement
mechanisms.

---

## Non-Claims Notice (Critical)

The TrustOrigin registry and this standard:

- do **not** verify or validate content
- do **not** certify authenticity or originality
- do **not** assert truth or accuracy
- do **not** adjudicate disputes
- do **not** provide legal, regulatory, or evidentiary opinions

All records produced by the registry confirm **existence at a point in time only**.

---

## Scope

The standard applies to registry operations involving:

- timestamped artefact references
- cryptographic hash custody
- declared metadata capture
- system event recording
- trust receipt issuance

It does not define evidentiary sufficiency or legal standing.

---

## Official Standard

The canonical version of the TrustOrigin Internal Standard is published at:

https://trustorigin.org/standards/internal-standard.html

This repository mirrors that document for reference and citation purposes.

## Use Cases (Non-Exhaustive)

- Synthetic media provenance

- Music and digital rights disputes

## Governance

TrustOrigin operates under a strict non-claims governance model.
The registry is designed to preserve neutrality and avoid implied endorsement
or validation.
